Rawakesari Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup Bombay rawa (semolina)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 2 tablespoons chopped cashews
  • 2 tablespoons raisins
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • A pinch of salt
  • Food color (optional, for a vibrant look)

Preparation:

  1. In a heavy-bottomed pan, heat 2 tablespoons of ghee. Add the Bombay rawa and roast on low flame until it turns golden brown and gives a nice aroma. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil.
  3. Once the water is boiling, add the sugar and a pinch of salt. Stir until the sugar dissolves completely.
  4. Gradually add the roasted rawa to the boiling water while continuously stirring to prevent lumps from forming.
  5. Cook on low flame, stirring continuously until the mixture thickens and the rawa absorbs the water completely.
  6. Add the remaining ghee, cardamom powder, and saffron strands (if using). Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes until the ghee starts to separate.
  7. In a small pan, heat a little ghee and fry the chopped cashews and raisins until golden. Add this to the kesari and mix well.
  8. Allow the kesari to cool slightly before serving. It can be enjoyed warm or at room temperature.
